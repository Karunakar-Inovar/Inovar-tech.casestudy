The Problem in Numbers

The Unified Care Team operated with a $26.6M annual budget and no integrated platform to track encampment locations, manage field workflows, and report program outcomes in real time. Decisions that should take minutes were taking days. Accountability to elected officials and the public was constrained by data the City couldn't reliably produce.

InovarTech developed and deployed NavApp — a purpose-built mobile platform giving Seattle's operational teams real-time geo-fenced mapping, structured encampment workflows, and an integrated analytics layer. For the first time, department heads, field supervisors, and the Mayor's office shared a single, live view of encampment status across the city.

The Situation
Eight encampment removals a day
with no real-time operational platform

Seattle's homelessness challenge is among the most acute of any U.S. city. With an estimated 16,385 people experiencing homelessness in King County — a 23% increase since 2022 — and over 9,800 sleeping unsheltered, the scale of the operational challenge dwarfs most peer cities. The Unified Care Team, the city's cross-departmental body for encampment management, was executing removals at a rate that tripled between 2022 and 2023.

But the city's data infrastructure had not kept pace with its operational tempo. Three specific failures defined the environment NavApp was built to solve:

  • Data fragmentation. No single source of truth on encampment locations. Field staff used disconnected spreadsheets and paper-based records. Public records requests revealed duplication rates as high as 10% between the city's own tracking logs — a sign of manual, inconsistent data entry across departments.
  • Manual coordination overhead. Encampment removal workflows lacked structure, automation, or escalation logic. Field supervisors, program managers, and elected officials could not access real-time status updates. Reports were compiled manually after the fact — not actionable for same-day decisions.
  • No outcome visibility. The $26.6M annual UCT budget produced no unified analytics on program effectiveness. Without integrated reporting, the City could not reliably answer basic accountability questions: Which neighborhoods had the highest encampment recurrence? Which sites had been resolved vs. returned? Where were shelter connections actually happening?
Strategic Diagnosis

The operational failure wasn't capacity — Seattle was executing at a high operational tempo. The failure was information architecture: the City lacked the real-time field visibility, structured workflows, and integrated analytics to deploy that capacity effectively.

The NavApp Solution
A purpose-built platform for government encampment operations

InovarTech designed NavApp from the ground up for the specific operational requirements of a municipal encampment management program — not adapted from a nonprofit case management tool or a general-purpose GIS application. The platform delivered three integrated capabilities:

01
Geo-Fenced Encampment Mapping
NavApp established precise, real-time geographic boundaries for every active encampment across Seattle. Unlike static maps or periodic survey snapshots, boundaries update as conditions change on the ground — giving the Unified Care Team an accurate operational picture at all times. Field workers can create, modify, or close encampment zones from a mobile device without returning to a desktop.
02
End-to-End Workflow Automation
Every encampment lifecycle stage — identification, inspection, prioritization, outreach engagement, service connection, and resolution — is tracked within a structured workflow. Automated escalation rules flag high-hazard sites for priority action. Removal records auto-populate from field data, eliminating the manual spreadsheet reconciliation that had previously produced duplication rates as high as 10% in the City's own records.
03
Real-Time Analytics & BI Layer
NavApp's integrated data architecture — from field collection through data warehouse and data lake to live BI dashboards — gives department heads and elected officials a single, reliable view of encampment trends, resource deployment, and program outcomes. Machine learning models surface patterns across neighborhoods and time periods, enabling proactive resource allocation rather than reactive response.
Results
Operational intelligence in the field
accountability data in the boardroom

NavApp's deployment transformed the City's encampment operations across four dimensions:

Unified operational picture

Encampment tracking consolidated onto a single geo-fenced platform, eliminating the multi-spreadsheet data reconciliation that had produced duplicate records. Field staff now enter data once, from mobile, and it propagates through the entire reporting chain.

Faster resolution cycle

Structured workflow automation reduced the time from encampment identification to first outreach contact by 35% by ensuring that escalation rules, notification triggers, and assignment workflows execute automatically — not through manual follow-up.

Real-time executive visibility

Department heads and the Mayor's office access live dashboards showing encampment status by neighborhood, resolution rates, outreach contacts made, and shelter connections achieved — in real time, not in quarterly reports.

Program accountability

NavApp's analytics layer generates the data the City needs to answer public accountability questions: encampment recurrence rates, service uptake percentages, program cost-per-resolution. The $26.6M UCT budget now has a reporting infrastructure commensurate with its scale.

What Makes NavApp Different
Built for government operations, not adapted from it

The market for homelessness technology is dominated by platforms designed for nonprofit service providers managing individual client records under federal HMIS requirements. NavApp operates from a fundamentally different design premise: the client is a city government, the primary unit of analysis is the encampment — not the individual case — and the accountability audience is elected officials and the public, not HUD program officers.

Government-First Architecture

Enterprise HMIS platforms are built for nonprofits and Continuums of Care navigating federal compliance. NavApp is built for municipal government operations — encampment management at scale, multi-department coordination, and public accountability dashboards. The architecture reflects who uses it.

Operations + Analytics in One Stack

Existing solutions require separate tools for field operations, case management, and analytics — often stitched together through manual exports. NavApp delivers field data capture, workflow management, and live BI dashboards through a single integrated platform, eliminating reconciliation overhead.

Encampment as Primary Use Case

Most market solutions treat encampment tracking as a module within a broader case management platform — an add-on to a system designed primarily for shelter intake and housing placement. For NavApp, the encampment is the primary unit of analysis: its boundaries, its population, its resolution status, and its program outcomes.

Machine Learning as Standard Infrastructure

Predictive analytics — recurrence modeling, demand forecasting, anomaly detection — are embedded in NavApp's architecture, not available as a premium add-on. For a city executing 2,800+ removals annually, the ability to anticipate where and when resources will be needed is operationally significant.
